# Glossary of Terms (Metasploit Framework)

## A

**Auxiliary Module**  
A Metasploit module used for scanning, enumeration, fuzzing, and information gathering. It does not exploit systems or create sessions.

---

**Attack Chain**  
A sequence of steps an attacker follows, typically including reconnaissance, exploitation, and post-exploitation.

---

## B

**Bind Shell**  
A type of shell where the target system opens a listening port, allowing the attacker to connect to it.

---

**Backdoor**  
A hidden method of accessing a system, often created after successful exploitation.

---

## C

**CVE (Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures)**  
A standardized identifier for known security vulnerabilities.

---

**Command Shell**  
A basic shell providing command-line access to a compromised system.

---

## E

**Exploit**  
Code or technique used to take advantage of a vulnerability in a system or application.

---

**Enumeration**  
The process of extracting detailed information about a target system, such as users, services, and configurations.

---

## L

**LHOST (Local Host)**  
The IP address of the attacker machine used in reverse connections.

---

**LPORT (Local Port)**  
The port on the attacker machine used to receive incoming connections.

---

## M

**Meterpreter**  
An advanced Metasploit payload that provides an interactive, in-memory command environment on the target system.

---

**Module**  
A self-contained component in Metasploit that performs a specific function (exploit, payload, auxiliary, post, encoder).

---

## P

**Payload**  
Code executed on the target system after a successful exploit. It defines what happens next (e.g., shell access).

---

**Post-Exploitation**  
Actions performed after gaining access to a system, such as privilege escalation or credential extraction.

---

**Privilege Escalation**  
The process of gaining higher-level permissions on a compromised system.

---

## R

**RHOSTS (Remote Hosts)**  
The target IP address or range of IPs in a Metasploit module.

---

**Reconnaissance**  
The phase of gathering information about a target system or network before exploitation.

---

## S

**Session**  
An active connection established between Metasploit and a compromised system.

---

**Shell**  
A command-line interface used to interact with a system. Can be basic or advanced (Meterpreter).

---

**Scanner Module**  
A type of auxiliary module used to detect services, ports, and vulnerabilities.

---

## T

**Target**  
A system or device being tested or attacked in a penetration testing scenario.

---

## V

**Vulnerability**  
A weakness or flaw in a system that can be exploited by an attacker.

---

## W

**Workspace**  
A feature in Metasploit used to organize different penetration testing projects and datasets.

---

## Z

**Zero-Day**  
A vulnerability that is unknown to the vendor and has no available patch.

---

## Summary

This glossary provides quick definitions of key terms used throughout Metasploit and penetration testing workflows. Understanding these concepts is essential for interpreting tools, commands, and attack scenarios effectively.
